Automation Bias ➡ The Hallucination Threat

Bias: The instinct to blindly trust system-generated reports to meet tight deadlines.

Risk: Accepting incorrect, hallucinatory AI outputs as factual, leading to flawed executive decisions.

📊

Confirmation Bias ➡ The Skewed Data Threat

Bias: Giving weight only to evidence that supports preconceived hypotheses.

Risk: Training models on narrow, incomplete demographics, generating biased outputs that alienate users.

⚠

Overconfidence ➡ The Verification Failure

Bias: Believing past success guarantees future accuracy.

Risk: Failing to implement third-party fact-checking on AI outputs, exposing the organization to deepfakes and misinformation.

🧠

Groupthink ➡ The Ethical Blindspot

Bias: Rushing to consensus without sufficient debate.

Risk: System justification that overrides ethical safeguards, prioritizing short-term tech adoption over long-term creative integrity and workforce reskilling.
